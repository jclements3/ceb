**Document ID: DI-MISC-80005C**  (or DI-MISC-80011B if emphasis is on hand tool aspect)

## 1. Scope

1.1 This performance specification establishes the requirements for a manually operated Compressed Earth Brick (CEB) Press, used for on-site production of standardized earth bricks in construction projects. The CEB press is designed to be sustainable, cost-effective, and suitable for regions where access to conventional building materials may be limited.

## 2. Referenced Documents

2.1 The following documents, of the issue in effect on the date of the solicitation, form a part of this specification to the extent specified herein:

*   **ASTM D698:** Standard Test Methods for Laboratory Compaction Characteristics of Soil Using Standard Effort (12,400 ft-lbf/ft³ (600 kN-m/m³))
*   **ASTM D1557:** Standard Test Methods for Laboratory Compaction Characteristics of Soil Using Modified Effort (56,000 ft-lbf/ft³ (2,700 kN-m/m³))

## 3. Requirements

### 3.1 Applicable Documents

The following documents, of the issue in effect on the date of the solicitation, form a part of this specification to the extent specified herein:

### 3.2 Requirements

#### 3.2.1 General

*   The CEB press shall be a manually operated, lever-action press designed for the compaction of soil mixtures into standardized brick shapes.
*   The press shall be lightweight (not to exceed [specify weight] kg), portable, and easy to assemble and disassemble without special tools for transportation and on-site use.

#### 3.2.2 Materials and Construction

*   The press shall be constructed of durable materials resistant to corrosion and wear, suitable for outdoor use in varying environmental conditions (-20°C to 50°C).
*   Steel components shall be treated with a rust-inhibiting coating compliant with [specify standard, e.g., ISO 12944].
*   All fasteners shall be corrosion-resistant stainless steel.

#### 3.2.3 Design

*   The press shall incorporate an ergonomic lever mechanism with a mechanical advantage of at least [specify value] for applying compressive force.
*   The mold shall be readily interchangeable to produce bricks of standardized dimensions (length: [specify], width: [specify], height: [specify]), ensuring consistent density and compressive strength.
*   The mold shall be easily removable for cleaning and maintenance, requiring no tools.
*   The press shall incorporate a reliable mechanism for ejecting the compacted brick from the mold, minimizing damage risk.

#### 3.2.4 Performance

*   The press shall be capable of producing bricks with a minimum compressive strength of [specify value] MPa, as determined by ASTM D698 or D1557.
*   The press shall be capable of producing bricks with a density of [specify value] kg/m³.
*   The press shall be capable of producing at least [specify value] bricks per hour with a single operator under normal operating conditions.

#### 3.2.5 Safety

*   The press shall comply with all applicable safety standards and regulations.
*   The press shall be stable during operation on level ground, with a tip-over angle of at least [specify value] degrees.
*   Moving parts shall be guarded or shielded to prevent accidental contact.
*   The lever shall incorporate a positive locking mechanism to prevent unintentional release during operation.

## 4. Quality Assurance Provisions

4.1 The CEB press shall be inspected and tested by the manufacturer to ensure compliance with all specified requirements.

4.2 The manufacturer shall provide a warranty for the press, covering defects in materials and workmanship for a period of [specify duration] from the date of delivery.

## 5. Preparation for Delivery

5.1 The CEB press shall be cleaned, lubricated (if applicable), and prepared for shipment.
5.2 The press shall be disassembled into its major components for packaging and transportation to minimize shipping volume.
5.3 A comprehensive user manual shall be included, providing detailed instructions for assembly, operation, maintenance, troubleshooting, and parts list with diagrams.

## 6. Notes

6.1 This performance specification provides the minimum requirements. Proposals exceeding these requirements may be considered favorably.
6.2 The government reserves the right to conduct verification testing to confirm compliance with this specification.

Ideally, the final brick size should be slightly smaller than 6x12 inches. certainly no bigger. This
is to make room for a little mortar or sealant. Approximately 1/16" off each dimension is
suggested. Grinding or milling 1/32 inches off both sides of the vertical channels is one possible
solution. Milling slots into the side plates would work also.
