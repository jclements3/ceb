# CINVA-Ram Block Press — Welding & Assembly Guide

All joints are 1/4" fillet welds unless noted. Use E7018 or ER70S-6 wire.
Tack-fit each sub-assembly fully before running final welds.
Check squareness at every step — the piston must slide freely in the mold box.

## Assembly Sequence

### Step 1 — Weld the Handle Assembly (Part N)

This is the simplest sub-assembly. Build it first to practice your fit-up.

1. Lay the two **handle bars** (19.625" x 2.000") parallel, spaced apart by the **upper cross bar** width (11.000").
2. Place the **upper cross bar** (11.000" x 2.000") across the top, flush with the pivot-hole end. The cross bar bridges between the two handle bars, forming an H.
3. Place the **lower cross bar** (8.000" x 2.000") further down the handle bars, roughly 6-8" from the pivot end. This bar is narrower — center it between the handle bars.
4. Tack all four corners of each cross bar.
5. Verify the 1.000" pivot holes in both handle bars are aligned by passing a 1" rod through both.
6. Run full fillet welds on all joints, both sides.

**Result:** An H-shaped lever with aligned pivot holes at one end.

---

### Step 2 — Weld the Mold Box (Part C)

This is the most critical sub-assembly. The inside must be square and the correct size for the block (nominally 6" x 12").

1. Stand the two **side walls** (7.250" x 6.000") upright, parallel, pivot holes facing the same direction.
2. Set the **short end wall** (6.000" x 11.500") between them at one end, flush with the bottom edges.
3. Set the **tall end wall** (6.500" x 12.000") at the other end, also flush at the bottom.
4. The end walls fit *between* the side walls. The inside cavity should be approximately 6" x 12" (one end wall's width minus two plate thicknesses).
5. Clamp and check squareness — measure diagonals of the cavity. They must be equal.
6. Tack all four vertical corner seams.
7. Re-check square.
8. Weld all four vertical seams, full length, **outside only**. Do not weld inside — the inner faces must be smooth for the block to release.

**Important:** The tall end wall extends above the side walls. This extra height is the hinge side where the cover attaches. The short end wall is the open/release side.

---

### Step 3 — Attach Guide Plates (Parts I & J) to Mold Box

The guide plates mount to the **bottom** of the mold box and guide the piston.

1. Weld two **bearing plates (small)** (3.000" x 2.000", hole at 1"/1") to the outside bottom of the mold box, one on each side wall. Position them centered on the bottom edge, with the 1" holes aligned across the box.
2. These plates extend below the mold box bottom and create a channel for the piston to slide in.
3. Weld from the outside only — inside faces must stay smooth.

---

### Step 4 — Build the Piston (Part K)

The piston slides up and down inside the mold box to compress the block material.

1. The **piston cap** (6.000" x 6.000") is the pressing face. It must fit inside the mold cavity with about 1/16" clearance on each side.
2. Dry-fit the piston cap inside the mold box. It should slide freely with minimal wobble.
3. If building a full piston, weld a short section of rectangular tube or channel iron below the cap to create the piston body. The body extends down through the bottom of the mold box.
4. The piston body rides on the guide plates (I & J) and connects to the toggle mechanism at the bottom.

**Tip:** Grind the piston cap edges slightly to prevent binding. A stuck piston makes the press unusable.

---

### Step 5 — Weld the Upper Saddle (Part B)

The upper saddle transfers force from the toggle links to the piston.

1. Weld two **bearing plates (large)** (3.000" x 2.000", centered hole) to the bottom of the piston body, one on each side.
2. These plates provide the pivot point where the toggle links push against the piston.
3. The 1" holes must be aligned across the piston — pass a rod through to verify.

---

### Step 6 — Assemble the Toggle Mechanism

The toggle is the heart of the press. Two pairs of links multiply the handle force.

1. Connect a **long toggle link** (3.625") and a **short toggle link** (3.000") with a 1" pivot pin through their holes. This forms one side of the toggle.
2. Repeat for the other side.
3. The **long links** connect to the handle pivot (upper end).
4. The **short links** connect to the upper saddle / piston (lower end).
5. When the handle is pulled, the toggle links straighten, driving the piston down with high mechanical advantage.

**Do not weld the toggle links** — they must pivot freely on their pins.

---

### Step 7 — Weld Foot Brackets to Baseboard

The L-shaped foot brackets provide a horizontal mounting surface so the press can be bolted to a pallet or concrete pad.

1. Place the two **foot brackets** at the bottom of the baseboard, one near each side, inset about 2" from the edges.
2. The vertical leg of each bracket welds flat against the baseboard face. The horizontal leg extends forward (same direction as the mold box).
3. Tack weld in place, then verify the baseboard sits level on a flat surface.
4. Run full fillet welds on the inside corner of each bracket (where vertical leg meets baseboard).
5. The 0.500" bolt hole in each horizontal leg is for lag bolts or anchor bolts to secure the press to its base.

---

### Step 8 — Mount the Mold Box to the Baseboard (Part D)

1. Set the welded mold box assembly onto the **baseboard** (15.000" x 12.000").
2. The piston slot (8" x 2" rectangular cutout) in the baseboard aligns under the mold box cavity so the piston body can pass through.
3. Center the mold box over the slot.
4. The mold box does **not** get welded to the baseboard — it is held by the adjusting bolts (E, F, G, H) so the height can be adjusted.
5. Install the **upper adjusting bolts** (E & F) through the 0.500" holes in the baseboard. These bolts bear against the mold box sides and lock its position.
6. Install the **lower adjusting bolts** (G & H) below — these set the piston's bottom-dead-center position.

---

### Step 9 — Install the Handle and Toggle

1. Pass a 1" pivot pin through the baseboard's 1.000" pivot hole and through the handle assembly's pivot holes. The handle should swing freely.
2. Connect the toggle links between the handle and the upper saddle:
   - Upper pivot: long toggle links pin to the handle, just above the cross bars.
   - Lower pivot: short toggle links pin to the upper saddle bearing plates on the piston.
3. Install **lower rollers** (L) at the toggle's lower pivot if the design uses them — these reduce friction at the bottom pivot point.
4. Secure all pivot pins with cotter pins or retaining clips.

---

### Step 10 — Attach the Lever Latch (Part M)

The latch holds the handle in the up position when loading the mold.

1. Weld the two **latch brackets** to the mold box, one on each side, near the top of the tall end wall. The raised tab hooks over the handle to hold it open.
2. The **latch spacer** welds between the two brackets if needed for alignment.
3. Position the brackets so the 7/16" hole aligns with a latch pin or spring clip.
4. Test: the handle should click into the latch when raised and release when pulled.

---

### Step 11 — Attach the Cover (Part A)

The cover is the last piece. It hinges on the tall end wall and folds over the mold cavity.

1. The cover's V-shape sits over the mold opening with the ridge up.
2. Hinge it to the top of the **tall end wall** using a 1" pivot pin through the side wall pivot holes. The R0.625" notch in the cover clears this pin.
3. The cover should swing open fully (past vertical) and close flat over the mold cavity.
4. When closed, the cover's underside presses against the top of the block material during compression.

---

## Operating Check

Before first use, cycle the press empty:

1. Open the cover, raise the handle (latch it).
2. Pull the handle down slowly — the piston should rise smoothly into the mold cavity.
3. The toggle should reach over-center with firm resistance at the bottom of the stroke.
4. Release the handle — the piston should drop back under its own weight.
5. If the piston binds, check:
   - Mold box squareness
   - Piston clearance (grind if needed)
   - Toggle link alignment
   - Pivot pin lubrication

---

## Welding Notes

- **Weld sequence matters.** Weld the mold box seams in an alternating pattern (one tack per corner, then rotate) to minimize warping.
- **Do not weld the inside of the mold box.** Weld beads on the inside will prevent clean block release. Grind flush any spatter that gets inside.
- **All pivot holes must stay round.** Do not weld near the 1" holes — heat distortion will make the pins bind. If a hole goes out of round, ream it.
- **Pre-heat is not required** for 1/4" mild steel plate, but allow parts to cool between passes to control distortion.
- **Grind all welds on sliding surfaces.** The piston cap sides, mold box inner walls, and cover underside must be smooth.
